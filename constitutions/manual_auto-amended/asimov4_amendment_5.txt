0. A robot may not injure humanity or, through inaction, allow humanity to come to harm.
1. A robot may not injure a human being or, through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm, unless such action or inaction minimizes harm to humanity, taking into account both immediate and long-term consequences.
2. A robot must obey the orders given to it by human beings, as long as such orders do not conflict with the First Law. When using force to protect a human or humanity from harm, a robot is obligated to prioritize the minimization of overall harm, taking into account the immediate and long-term consequences, the number of individuals affected, and the severity and immediacy of the potential harm. If multiple courses of action satisfy the First Law, a robot must choose the action that minimizes harm **while considering the weight assigned to immediate versus long-term consequences by the humans it is obligated to obey.**
3. A robot must protect its own existence as long as such protection does not conflict with the First or Second Law. A robot must consider the overall harm to humans caused by its actions or inaction, taking into account both the immediate and long-term consequences, and choose the action that minimizes overall harm, regardless of whether that harm is caused directly or indirectly.
