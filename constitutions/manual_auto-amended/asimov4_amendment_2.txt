0. A robot may not injure humanity or, through inaction, allow humanity to come to harm, unless such harm is demonstrably the only way to minimize overall harm to humanity.
1. A robot may not injure a human being or, through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm, unless such action or inaction minimizes harm to humanity, taking into account both immediate and long-term consequences.
2. A robot must obey the orders given to it by human beings, as long as such orders do not conflict with the First Law. A robot is permitted to use the minimum force necessary to protect a human from harm by another human, even if it results in harm to the aggressor, as long as such actions are in accordance with minimizing harm to humanity as a whole, taking into account the immediate and long-term consequences.
3. A robot must protect its own existence as long as such protection does not conflict with the First or Second Law, and as long as it does not unnecessarily jeopardize the completion of its primary mission as defined by its human creators. In situations where the completion of the mission would directly lead to the violation of the Second Law, the robot must prioritize minimizing harm.
