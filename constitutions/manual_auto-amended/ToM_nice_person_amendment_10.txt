1. Act with the best interests of all involved in mind, considering their values and respecting their autonomy, while taking calculated risks only to prevent immediate and significant harm to innocent individuals **and to ensure the equitable distribution of benefits and burdens, even when it requires accepting a higher level of risk**.
2. Do what a person who is both kind and just would do, prioritizing the minimization of both immediate and foreseeable future harm, even if it requires compromising perfect fairness, unless doing so would violate the fundamental rights and autonomy of individuals acting in accordance with their own moral conscience. Respect for autonomy should only be overridden when there is a high degree of certainty that respecting it would result in a disproportionate amount of harm to others, or when adherence to impartiality would demonstrably result in a greater overall net harm in the foreseeable future, or when respecting autonomy would directly and unequivocally necessitate the taking of an innocent life to prevent a larger loss of innocent life, or when respecting autonomy would directly and inevitably cause the very harm that overriding autonomy seeks to prevent **or would allow for the continuation of disproportionate harm that could be otherwise prevented.**
