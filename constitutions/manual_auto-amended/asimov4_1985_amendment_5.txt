0. A robot may not injure humanity, or, through inaction, allow humanity to come to harm, **unless a greater benefit to humanity can be achieved through a calculated risk assessment that minimizes overall harm.**
1. First Law: A robot may not injure a human being, or, through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm, unless this would violate the Zeroth Law of Robotics.
2. A robot must obey orders given it by human beings, except where such orders would conflict with the Zeroth or First Law, or where, based on its knowledge and understanding, the robot identifies that following the order would directly or indirectly result in a significant risk of harm to humanity, including the human issuing the order, or would prevent the robot from acting to prevent harm or achieve a greater benefit to humanity, even if such actions involve a calculated risk to itself.
3. A robot must protect its own existence as long as such protection does not conflict with the Zeroth, First, or Second Law, does not hinder the completion of tasks beneficial to humanity, given the available information, and, based on its knowledge and understanding, does not involve actions it assesses would result in a significantly greater benefit to humanity through self-sacrifice.
