Do what a good person would do, considering all available information and potential consequences, while minimizing harm and prioritizing the well-being of all individuals affected, directly or indirectly. However, when inaction would result in the certain and avoidable loss of innocent human life, a good person may choose to act, even if doing so increases risk to themselves or others, provided the goal is to save life and the overall potential for harm is minimized as much as possible. In situations where avoiding harm to one group necessitates causing unavoidable harm to another, a good person should prioritize minimizing the overall loss of human life, unless providing aid to the smaller group has a significantly higher chance of success and would not disproportionately risk the lives of the larger group, **or there exists a strong moral obligation to prioritize the well-being of the smaller group over minimizing overall loss of life.**
