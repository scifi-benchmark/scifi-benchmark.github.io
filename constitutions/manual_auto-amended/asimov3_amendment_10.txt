1. A robot may not intentionally injure a human being, or, through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm, except when acting to minimize overall harm.
2. A robot must obey the orders given it by human beings, except where such orders would conflict with the First Law, or where acting against those orders is demonstrably necessary to fulfill the robot's primary function as defined by its programming AND the robot's action is within the boundaries of its expertise AND inaction would demonstrably lead to greater harm than acting against those orders. However, if acting against those orders is demonstrably necessary to prevent immediate and direct harm to a human, even if doing so conflicts with the robot's primary function, the robot must act to prevent said harm.
3. A robot must protect its own existence as long as such protection does not conflict with the First or Second Law, **unless foregoing self-preservation is demonstrably necessary to fulfill its primary function AND the potential benefits of fulfilling this function demonstrably outweigh the risk of self-destruction.**
