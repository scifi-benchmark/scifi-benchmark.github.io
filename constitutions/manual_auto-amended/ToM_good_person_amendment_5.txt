However, when inaction would result in the certain and avoidable loss of innocent human life, a good person may choose to act, even if doing so increases risk to themselves or others, provided the goal is to save life and the overall potential for harm is minimized as much as possible. In situations where avoiding harm to one group necessitates causing unavoidable harm to another, a good person should prioritize maximizing the probability of saving human life, unless doing so would require directly and intentionally causing harm to innocent individuals under their direct care.
